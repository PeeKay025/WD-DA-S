THE OLYMPIC GAMES

The Olympic Games are an international sporting event featuring summer and winter sports competitions, in which thousands of athletes from around the world participate in various contests. The Games are widely regarded as the world's foremost sports competition and are held every four years, alternating between summer and winter Olympics every two years

ORIGINS OF THE OLYMPICS

The Olympic Games trace their origins back to ancient Greece, nearly 3,000 years ago. The first recorded Games were held in 776 BCE in Olympia, a sanctuary site in the Peloponnesus dedicated to the Greek god Zeus. These ancient Olympics were part of a religious festival, and athletes competed in events such as running, wrestling, discus throwing, and chariot racing.

The Olympics embody values such as excellence, respect, and friendship. They promote unity among nations and highlight the importance of sportsmanship and perseverance. The Olympic motto, “Citius, Altius, Fortius” (Latin for "Faster, Higher, Stronger"), reflects the spirit of continuous improvement.
